2. What Are Cookies and Tracking Technologies

2.1 Definition of Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are used to store small pieces of information. They are stored on your device when the website is loaded on your browser. Cookies help us make the website function properly, make it more secure, provide better user experience, and understand how the website performs.

2.2 Types of Cookies by Duration

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Permanent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until deleted
  • Flash Cookies: Also known as Local Shared Objects, used by Flash applications

2.3 Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use:

  • Web Beacons: Small transparent images that track page views
  • Pixels: Tiny invisible images used for tracking and analytics
  • Local Storage: HTML5 storage for client-side data persistence
  • Session Storage: Temporary browser storage for session data
  • Fingerprints: Device and browser characteristic collection

3. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for several reasons, categorized by their purpose and necessity:

3.1 Essential Cookies (Strictly Necessary)

Required for the website to function properly. They cannot be switched off in our systems.

  • Authentication: Maintain login sessions and user authentication
  • Security: Protect against CSRF attacks and malicious activities
  • Load Balancing: Distribute traffic across servers for optimal performance
  • Form Data: Temporarily store form data during completion

3.2 Analytics Cookies (Performance)

Help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously.

  • Google Analytics: Tracks page views, user behavior, and conversion metrics
  • Heat Mapping: Records mouse movements and scroll behavior
  • Performance Monitoring: Measures site speed and user experience
  • Error Tracking: Identifies and reports website issues

3.3 Functionality Cookies (Preferences)

Enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalisation.

  • Language Settings: Remember your preferred language
  • Theme Preferences: Store dark/light mode preferences
  • Layout Settings: Remember customized interface preferences
  • Search History: Store recent searches for quick access

3.4 Marketing Cookies (Targeting)

Used to deliver relevant advertisements and track the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.

  • Ad Targeting: Show personalized advertisements based on interests
  • Social Media: Enable social media sharing and integration
  • Retargeting: Display relevant ads on other websites
  • Affiliate Tracking: Track referrals and affiliate conversions

6. Managing Cookies and Your Choices

6.1 Browser Settings

You can control and manage cookies in various ways. Please note that removing or blocking cookies can negatively affect your user experience and parts of our website may no longer be fully accessible.

6.2 How to Control Cookies

Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser setting to decline cookies if you prefer:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions

6.3 Our Cookie Management Tool

We provide a cookie consent management tool that allows you to:

  • View all cookies used on our website
  • Enable or disable non-essential cookies
  • Customize your cookie preferences
  • Withdraw consent at any time

6.4 Global Privacy Control

We honor the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal:

  • If GPC is enabled in your browser, we will treat it as a cookie opt-out
  • GPC signal takes precedence over other cookie settings
  • You can enable GPC in supported browsers and extensions

7. Technical Details and Implementation

7.1 Cookie Attributes

Our cookies use the following standard attributes:

  • Domain: Specifies which domains can access the cookie
  • Path: Specifies which pages can access the cookie
  • Secure: Ensures cookies are only sent over HTTPS
  • HttpOnly: Prevents JavaScript access for security
  • SameSite: Controls cross-site request behavior

7.2 First-Party vs Third-Party Cookies

  • First-Party Cookies: Set directly by our website domain
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by domains other than the one you're visiting
  • Session Cookies: Exist only while browser is open
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device after browser closes

7.3 Local Storage and Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use:

  • Local Storage: Stores data with no expiration date
  • Session Storage: Stores data for browser session only
  • IndexedDB: Client-side database for larger data storage
  • WebSQL: Legacy client-side database (deprecated)
